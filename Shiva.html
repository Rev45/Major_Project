Shiva Daksa
Historical Text:

The text recounts a sacrificial assembly where Dakṣa, filled with pride and envy, insults Lord Śiva despite being welcomed respectfully by others. Dakṣa's disrespect leads to a series of curses and counter-curses between the followers of Lord Śiva and Dakṣa's party. Dakṣa's envy and lack of understanding of transcendental knowledge are highlighted through his actions and subsequent curses. Ultimately, Lord Śiva, saddened by the conflict, leaves the assembly without saying anything. The text emphasizes the importance of sacrifice as a means to worship the Supreme Lord and warns against blasphemy of Vedic principles, which leads to atheism.
The story of the Dakṣa yajña involves Dakṣa, who was insulted by Lord Śiva and consequently conducted a grand sacrificial ritual. However, Dakṣa's daughter Satī, who was married to Lord Śiva, felt humiliated by her father's disrespect towards her husband and immolated herself. Enraged by this, Lord Śiva created a fierce demon from his hair, who then wreaked havoc at the sacrificial arena. Eventually, after various events and prayers from different celestial beings, the sacrifice was reinstated, and Dakṣa was forgiven by Lord Śiva. The story emphasizes the supremacy of Lord Viṣṇu and the importance of hearing and narrating this tale for spiritual purification.
Important Facts:

The text describes a heated argument between a father and his son-in-law, Shiva. The father criticizes Shiva for his lack of manners and hygiene, calling him shameless and impure. He disapproves of Shiva's lifestyle and appearance, contrasting it with his daughter's beauty and purity. The father reluctantly married his daughter to Shiva due to pressure from Brahma, but feels Shiva has dishonored him and his daughter.
